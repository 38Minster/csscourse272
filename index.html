Why Poems

Column 1

Actually this is not a site about poems so much as the shape of poems. Though there is a plan to include rhyming systems at some stage later on, and there will be a brief romp through them here these don't really float my boat in the way that the shapes of poems do - don't rush off in the way that most people do when the word poem is mentioned, think of these poems as a sort of Sudoku, or a poets version of a puzzle. Imagine that you have something to say, but you can only say it in a particular way. Of course it has to be worthwhile, and not a waste of anyone's time, and it would be nice, of course to move people too, just as the Divine Comedy was- Dante no doubt scratched his head a bit about what he was going to say, then he went and wrote it so that every middle line of the three-line stanzas it was made up of rhymed with the first and third lines of the next stanza - I'll show you how in the Terze Rima section (that is what this kind of rhyme is called).

What is here then are various puzzles that poets construct for themselves - ask them what a poem is, and they are not able to answer, or rather they do, but in so many ways as to keep the mystique. It goes without saying that my favourite definition is that of Leonard Cohen (an author of a Villanelle himself, who said "

Poetry is just the evidence of life. If your life is burning well, poetry is just the ash.
